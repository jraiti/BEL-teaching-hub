Designing for AI: Open vs Secure Assessment ‚ñº

Open Assessment (AI-Permitted)

Students can use AI tools transparently with proper acknowledgment. Design considerations:

  • Set clear expectations about AI acknowledgment requirements in course profile
  • Model effective AI use through scaffolded prompts and examples
  • Use Microsoft Copilot to simulate AI's role in completing tasks
  • Design tasks that require synthesis, application, and critical thinking beyond AI capabilities
  • Consider tasks that ask students to evaluate, critique, or improve AI-generated content

Secure Assessment (AI-Restricted)

Assessment designed to verify independent student learning without AI assistance:

  • Supervised tasks: In-class exams, invigilated assessments
  • Oral components: Viva voce, presentations with Q&A
  • Real-time demonstrations: Practical skills, live problem-solving
  • Scaffolded submissions: Work developed progressively in supervised settings

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Policy Update: From Semester 2, 2025, every course must include at least one secure assessment item. Plan your assessment strategy accordingly.

Secure Assessment Design

Ensuring academic integrity and authentic student performance

"Assessment is not about you as a teacher; it is about your students and their learning."
‚Äî Damian Cooper

üéØ UQ Assessment Security Framework

Security is assessed at the program/plan level to ensure graduating students have genuinely completed their studies. Not all assessment items need maximum security - the key is strategic planning across the program.

Policy Update üìÑ PPL 3.10.02 Assessment Procedures

Secure Assessment Strategies

  • Supervised Tasks: Invigilated exams and in-class assessments
  • Secure Questioning: Student discussions about submitted work in supervised conditions
  • Scaffolded Submission: Samples of work developed in supervised settings
  • Real-time Completion: Entire tasks completed under supervision
  • Viva Voce: Oral examination components (e.g., HLTH7315 approach)
  • Corroboration: Work verified by trusted observers (e.g., clinical placements)

Assessment Hurdles

Hurdles are assessment requirements that must be satisfied to receive a specific grade. Use the three principles:

1. Critical Competency & Security: Add hurdles only when essential for learning outcomes or to securely assure professional competencies

2. Reasonable Standards: Set achievable thresholds that demonstrate competency without unnecessary stress

3. Clear Communication: Explicitly state hurdle requirements, rationale, and consequences in course profiles from day one

Faculty-Specific Hurdle Guidance:

Check with your Faculty for specific hurdle implementation expectations. Examples include:

  • Medicine: Performance hurdles (minimum performance to progress) vs Compliance hurdles (engagement requirements)
  • Your Faculty: Contact your Associate Dean (Academic) for faculty-specific hurdle guidance

Assessment Security Guidelines - Full Framework ‚ñº

Teaching staff must give due regard to assessment integrity and security to minimise academic misconduct. Incorporate these elements:

  1. Design meaningful, relevant, and engaging assessment tasks
  2. Provide clear expectations about assessment requirements and academic integrity
  3. Use class time to discuss assessment scope, genre, and cognitive demands
  4. Identify situations where misconduct is likely and implement preventive approaches
  5. Support students who may find themselves under pressure
  6. Monitor assessment outcomes and effectiveness regularly
  7. Ensure assessment design prevents memorisation/reproduction from leading to passing grades

üìå Important Context - Don't Panic!

These are recommended guidelines for faculty-level program assessment, not mandatory requirements for every individual course. Here's what this means:

  • Program-level, not course-level: Security is evaluated across an entire program/plan, so individual courses can have different security levels
  • Faculty decides thresholds: Your Associate Dean (Academic), Heads of School, and program convenors set specific requirements for your faculty
  • Flexibility by design: The guidelines acknowledge that prioritising goals other than security is necessary for some assessment items
  • Not a universal mandate: Check with your Associate Dean (Academic) or program convenor for your specific program requirements

From Semester 2, 2025, the transition will begin to ensure that every course includes at least one secure assessment item - this is the main requirement for individual courses.

Program-Level Security Assessment

For program/plan convenors: Security is evaluated at the program/plan level using these recommended criteria (adjustable by faculty):

  • Program level: Minimum of 75% of core courses are secure
  • Program level: Minimum of 67% of flexible core courses are secure

Course-Level Security Options

A course is considered secure if it meets ONE of these three options:

  • Option 1: 30% high security assessment (by weighting) that is also a hurdle; OR
  • Option 2: 50% high security assessment (by weighting); OR
  • Option 3: 70% medium security assessment (by weighting)

How Course Coordinators Can Support Student Wellbeing ‚ñº

Practical Strategies:

  • Include support information in course materials: Add links to Student Central, counselling, and food support in your Learn.UQ site
  • Be aware of assessment pressure: Consider spacing due dates, providing extensions processes, and reducing unnecessary assessment stress
  • Recognise signs of distress: Students may struggle with attendance, engagement, or performance. Refer them to support services early
  • Normalise help-seeking: Mention available support in class, normalise that many students use these services
  • Be flexible when needed: Work with Accessibility and Welfare Advisers to implement Student Access Plans
  • Know your limits: You're not a counsellor - refer students to professional support services

‚ö†Ô∏è If You're Concerned About a Student:

Call UQ Counselling and Crisis Line: 1300 851 998

Request a welfare check (business hours): Student Support and Wellbeing

Staff Training: Complete the "Supporting students with mental health conditions" module in Workday for guidance on identifying and supporting students in distress.
